## Bad Angel Series ID card

**Title:** Bad Angel

**Type:** TV Series

**Genre:** Action, Crime, Cop Drama

**Inspiration:** 1980s Cop Dramas

**Series Summary:** In this modern day spin of the 80's cop dramas, God sends an angel disguised as an FBI special agent, to help another angel already stationed in Los Angeles fighting Satan's thralls in eternal fight of Good vs. Evil. But this new angel, isn't playing by the books.

## Angels

**1. Zephyr Kozinski:** Zephyr Kozinski is human name for Angel Zephyr, a young, energetic angel who breaks the old fashioned norms of behavior. Zephyr posses demi-god powers, in terms of strength and ability to warp time and reality. He's coming from a rough background, from a rough planet where bad things and bad choices come together. At some point in his youth Zephyr did something really bad that haunts his spirit even in the afterlife. Zephyr is a convert—meaning God offered him a path for redemption—proving that not all angels are the same.

**2. Clarence Grey:** Clarence Grey is human name for Angel Clarus, an old fashioned, everything by the book veteran. Clarence got vast general knowledge enhanced by Angelic mental powers. He is really smart, but jaded. He sees Earth as a mess, where core social values are distorted and crime is rampant. 

Both of our angels come from different realms of God. Clarence come from Silvana - a peaceful utopia, while Zephyr comes from Kryllos - a hellish mess. Our angel's origins are a metaphor for the generational gaps so very common among humans. Usually older generation romanticize the time of their own childhood (planet Silvana), while criticizing the younger generations (planet Kryllos). So our series will tackle social issues, religion, crime, politics, etc... and that dynamic will play part in the relationship between our main characters. Our two main characters will have a conflicting start. Clarence will see Zephyr as a loose cannon who'll get them both killed, but their relationship will grow into the one resembling a father and a son.

**Important questions and answers about Zephyr**
* **Why did God make Zephyr an Angel?** - Zephyr was a kid who had no choice in life. Born and lived in bad circumstances that only lead to bad decisions. If Zephyr had been born on Earth in a good family, he would've been a successful and great person. This should emphasize influence of environment on people. **Example:** There's a real case of switched babies in Japan, where hospital made a mistake and assigned babies to different families. A kid who was born to poor parents was given to the more affluent family and became successful in life. The kid from affluent family was given to the poor, and he had no choice in life that luxury provides, therefore he didn't do well. Not all people have the same choices in life!
* **Why did God send Zephyr to Earth?** - Because the same Devil's followers who killed Zephyr at Kryllos are now operating at Earth, in Los Angeles.
* **Was Zephyr at any point courted by the Devil?** - Yes. Remember, Zephyr is a convert. The Devil, recognized Zephyr's pain offered him a way to *control* it. A promise of strength, of agency, of a way to reshape his world – mirroring his desire for control born from Kryllos’ chaos.
* **How did Zephyr and the Devil fall out?** - Zephyr was never a bad person. Sometimes, bad choices in life are made for sheer survival and necessity. Despite his immense talent, he proved to be not worthy for the Devil, and Devil ordered him to be killed on Kryllos.
* **How much will Clarence know about Zephyr background** - In the beginning Clarence will know only what God approved to be known about Zephyr. He will know that Zephyr comes from Kryllos and that he's immensely capable but prone to breaking rules. Everything else, Clarence will find out...

**Core Character Conflicts:**
A significant source of conflict between Zephyr and Clarence stems from Zephyr's youthful enthusiasm for Earth and his desire to fully immerse himself in the human experience – which includes pursuing romantic relationships. While Zephyr sees his "bachelor" activities as a means to better understand humanity and even to blend into social circles where information might be found, Clarence views these pursuits as a dangerous distraction from their divine mission. Clarence sternly warns Zephyr of the celestial protocol against angels forming deep emotional bonds with mortals, fearing it could weaken Zephyr's angelic connection, compromise their mission, or even make him vulnerable to demonic influence. This tension escalates when Zephyr finds himself genuinely drawn to a human woman, a connection that not only challenges Clarence's rules but also potentially puts her in the crosshairs of Orion Kincaid's nefarious network, proving Clarence's warnings prophetic in the most perilous way. Zephyr's internal struggle to balance his burgeoning feelings with his divine duty will be a recurring theme, pushing the boundaries of his relationship with his jaded partner.

**Angelic Protocols & Heavenly Communications**

To maintain their mission and receive directives, Zephyr and Clarence must frequently establish contact with Heaven. This is achieved by attuning themselves to the concentrated spiritual energy found within any place of human worship – be it a church, mosque, synagogue, or other sacred site. These locations act as conduits, allowing them to communicate with a "Senior Angel" (their celestial 'precinct captain') who oversees their earthly operations.

This vital protocol becomes another rich source of friction and humor between the partners. Clarence, ever the jaded veteran, often uses these moments to critique human religious practices, lamenting what he perceives as hypocrisy, division, or a fundamental misunderstanding of the "Boss's" true message. He might sigh at the commercialization, the rigid dogmas, or the perceived superficiality of certain rituals. Zephyr, on the other hand, finds the entire spectacle endlessly amusing and fascinating. He often quips about the "Boss" having so many different "aliases" or "brand names" (Jesus, Allah, etc.) depending on the building, expressing a good-natured bewilderment at human complexity and the sheer variety of their attempts to connect with the divine. This contrast provides both comedic relief and poignant commentary on faith, seen through the eyes of those who serve the divine directly.

## Dialogue Style & Tone for Bad Angel

The dialogue in **Bad Angel** should be a dynamic blend of gritty police procedural realism, sharp, character-driven humor, and distinct generational/celestial friction. It should include fast-paced, overlapping exchanges that convey the high-pressure environment. Think of the following:
* **Grounded Cynicism and World-Weariness:** Humor will be born from cynicism and gallows humor. Jokes about the job, the brass, the criminals, or each other will be quick, cutting, and often darkly funny.
* **Sharp, Character-Driven Banter**
* **Understated Observations and Reactions**
* **Authentic "Cop Talk" with a Twist:** police talk should be authentic but not overly dense, punctuated by weary sighs, exasperated commands, and quick, sardonic remarks. It's the language of people who've seen too much and are using wit to stay sane.
* **Swearing and curse words are fine:** People curse under tense circumstances. It may not be polite but that's what happens all the time. So don't be shy to use curse words.

## Series Settings

Current day Los Angeles. Not only the very name of the city means "The Angels" in Spanish, but it's current complex problems are exactly why this city would need help from the "real" angels. Satan on the other side sees the city's problems such as crime, high rates of poverty, homelessness, etc... as a fertile ground to establish a strong foothold in this fight against God.
 
## Villain for Season One

**Orion Kincaid - The Kingpin of Vice:** a notoriously elusive and hyper-efficient criminal mastermind who has, over the past year, consolidated control over nearly all of Los Angeles's illicit trades – drugs, arms, human trafficking, and even high-tech cybercrime. He operates from the shadows, his face rarely seen, his orders enforced by a network of fiercely loyal and unusually ruthless lieutenants.

**What Orion Kincaid Wants:** Orion Kincaid is a human shell of a person from Kryllos who made a Faustian bargain, selling his soul for unprecedented criminal power and strategic genius. He is now a living conduit for a powerful demon, making him virtually unstoppable by conventional means. Kincaid's goal is to turn LA into his personal criminal empire, a self-sustaining ecosystem of vice and corruption where every illicit act generates negative energy and suffering, feeding his demonic patron. He aims to expand his dominion until the city itself becomes a monument to human depravity, an open portal for darker entities to enter the mortal realm.
